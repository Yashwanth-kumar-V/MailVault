Privacy Policy

We built MailVault with one principle: your emails belong to you, not us.
This page explains exactly what happens to your data.

Last updated: June 2026
✅
The core promise

MailVault runs entirely inside your browser. Your emails never leave your device. We have no servers, no database, and no way to see your data — even if we wanted to.

When you sign in with Google, your Gmail access token is held in your browser's memory for that session only. When you close the tab or sign out, the token is gone. Nothing is written to any external server.

📧
What we access from your Gmail

When you grant permission, MailVault uses the Gmail API to:

  • Read email metadata (sender, subject, date, size) to display your email list
  • Read full email content and attachments when you choose to download a backup
  • Move emails to Trash when you use the "Move to Trash" feature
  • Permanently delete Trash contents when you use the "Purge Trash" feature

All of this happens directly between your browser and Google's servers. MailVault acts as a bridge — it never stores, copies, or transmits your email content anywhere.

🚫
What we never do
Upload your emails to any server
Store your Gmail token persistently
Send emails on your behalf
Share data with third parties
Track your usage or behaviour
Use analytics or ad tracking
Access emails you didn't select
Retain any data after sign-out
💾
What is stored locally on your device

If you use the "Own API Key" option, your Google OAuth Client ID is saved in your browser's localStorage so you don't have to re-enter it each visit. This stays on your device only — we cannot access it.

Your Gmail access token is held in memory only and is never written to localStorage, cookies, or any storage that persists after the tab is closed.

The backup ZIP file is downloaded directly to your device's Downloads folder — it is never uploaded anywhere.

🔑
Google OAuth & permissions

MailVault uses Google's OAuth 2.0 system to authenticate you. We request the following Gmail scopes:

  • gmail.readonly — to read and list your emails
  • gmail.modify — to move emails to Trash
  • mail.google.com — to permanently delete Trash contents

You can revoke MailVault's access at any time by visiting myaccount.google.com/permissions and removing MailVault from the list of connected apps.

🌐
Third-party services

MailVault loads the following external resources to function:

  • Google Fonts (DM Sans) — for typography. Google may log this request per their own privacy policy.
  • Google Identity Services — for OAuth sign-in. Subject to Google's privacy policy.
  • Gmail API (googleapis.com) — for email access. All requests go directly from your browser to Google.
  • JSZip (cdnjs.cloudflare.com) — loaded on demand when you download a backup. Cloudflare may log this request.

No other third-party services, analytics tools, or advertising networks are used.

👶
Children's privacy

MailVault is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect any information from children. If you believe a child has used this service, please contact us so we can address the concern.

📋
Changes to this policy

If this privacy policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a new "Last updated" date. Since MailVault collects no data from you, any changes are likely to be minor clarifications rather than material changes to how your data is handled.
